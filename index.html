Ecosystem Fundamentals - Grade 9

Core Components

  • Producers: Organisms that make their own food (e.g., plants through photosynthesis)
  • Consumers:
    • Primary consumers (herbivores)
    • Secondary consumers (carnivores)
    • Tertiary consumers (apex predators)
  • Decomposers: Break down organic matter (e.g., fungi, bacteria)

Energy Flow

Food Chain Example:

Sun → Grass (Producer) → Grasshopper (Primary Consumer) → Frog (Secondary Consumer) → Snake (Tertiary Consumer)

  • Only 10% energy transfer between trophic levels
  • Energy pyramids show decreasing available energy at higher levels

Ecological Relationships

  • Symbiosis:
    • Mutualism (both benefit)
    • Commensalism (one benefits, other unaffected)
    • Parasitism (one benefits, other harmed)
  • Predator-prey dynamics
  • Competition for resources

Human Impacts

  • Deforestation effects on biodiversity
  • Pollution bioaccumulation in food chains
  • Climate change impacts on species distribution
  • Importance of sustainable practices

Key Terms

Biodiversity: Variety of life in an ecosystem

Habitat: Natural environment of an organism

Niche: Role of an organism in its ecosystem

Sample Test Questions

  1. Explain why energy pyramids are always upright
  2. Compare parasitism and commensalism with examples
  3. Calculate energy available at tertiary level if producers have 50,000 kcal
